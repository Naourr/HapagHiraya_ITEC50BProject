Classic Pandesal

Pandesal is the backbone of Filipino bakery culture. These iconic bread rolls are slightly sweet, wonderfully airy, and rolled in fine breadcrumbs before baking to create their signature textured crust. Best enjoyed fresh out of the oven, dipped straight into a hot cup of coffee or stuffed with cheese.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup white sugar & 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk & ¼ cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or vegetable oil
  • 1 egg, room temperature
  • ½ cup fine breadcrumbs

Instructions

  1. Dissolve the yeast and 1 tablespoon of sugar in warm water. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, remaining sugar, salt, warm milk, melted butter, egg, and the activated yeast mixture. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 8–10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Shape into a ball, place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1 hour.
  4. Punch down the dough and divide it into equal-sized small balls. Roll each ball generously in breadcrumbs and arrange them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Let rise again for 20 minutes.
  5. Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F) and bake the bread rolls for 15–20 minutes until the tops turn a beautiful golden brown color. Serve immediately.
