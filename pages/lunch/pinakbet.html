Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a classic, vibrant Northern Filipino vegetable stew flavored with savory shrimp paste. Featuring a hearty mix of local crops like bitter melon, eggplant, okra, and squash, this dish perfectly captures the comforting rustic flavors of traditional Filipino home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 200g pork belly, sliced into small pieces
  • 2 tablespoons bagoong alamang (shrimp paste)
  • 1 medium bitter melon (ampalaya), cored and sliced
  • 2 medium eggplants, sliced
  • 1 cup butternut squash (kalabasa), cubed
  • 6-8 pieces okra
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes in a large pot until soft.
  2. Add the pork belly and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add the shrimp paste and stir well, letting it cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the water or broth and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add the squash first and cook for 3 minutes until slightly softened.
  6. Add the remaining vegetables in order of cooking time (okra, bitter melon, string beans, eggplant).
  7. Cover and simmer over medium heat until all vegetables are tender but crisp.
  8. Serve hot with a steaming bowl of rice.
